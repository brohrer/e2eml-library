A sparse feature creator

Ziptie is a unsupervised feature creation algorithm that works by greedily agglomerating dimensions. It falls under the broad category of dimensionality reduction in the sense that it operates on dimensions (channels or colunms) of data rather than observations (measurements or rows). However, it can in fact result in an increase in dimensions. (The number of dimensions you end up with is a parameter you can set.)

What is ziptie good for?

sparsity interpretability generality. No assumptions about structure.
